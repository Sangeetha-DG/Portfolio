Food Stamp Participation Analysis in New Jersey (2022)

Data-Driven Public Assistance Study | By Sangeetha D G

Abstract

This thesis explores SNAP (Food Stamp) participation trends across different demographics in New Jersey using visual data analytics in Tableau. It aims to identify socio-economic patterns and disparities in public assistance distribution.

Project Metadata

  • Category: Data analysis and predictive modeling
  • Client: Academic
  • Date: 20 October 2023
  • Interactive Dashboard: Tableau Public Link

Objectives

  • Identify participation rates among NJ households receiving food stamps.
  • Compare distributions by poverty level, race, and work status.
  • Use visual storytelling to convey socio-economic trends.

Key Findings

  • 8.6% of all NJ households received SNAP benefits in 2022.
  • 85% of households below poverty received benefits vs. only 14% above.
  • Hispanic/Latino households had highest participation (22%).
  • 43% of single-worker households relied on SNAP despite employment.

Conclusion

The visualization-driven approach clearly highlights inequalities in access to food support and helps guide data-informed policymaking. It underscores the need for targeted outreach programs based on race, income, and work status indicators.
